## Style

The style of the game will be a charming and increasingly popular aesthetic "low-fi 3D" or "neo-retro". Blending the technical limitation of 1990s hardware with modern lighting and artistic sensibilities. 

### Low Poly Models

Models should have geometric simplicity, with low poly count. Objects portraed by simple shapes. Emulated PS1 era, creating a "toy-like" or "chibi" feel that ephasizes silhouette over complex detail. Because the models are simple, the movement (like a pig flopping onto its belly) feels more "snappy" and expressive.

### Low Resolution Textures

The textures used are purposfully pixelated. The Texel density should display individually, in pixels, instead of having smooth, high-definition skins. Blush should be flate planes with simple pixel art textures, giving it a handcrafted indie feel.

### Vertex snapping & wobble

Edges of models should have vertex jitter, a technical artifact of PS1 which didn't have floating-point math.

### Stylized lighting and color palette.

Despite the retro technical constraits, the lighting is modern.

Color Grading - It should be bright, saturaed, and have high contrast of a "dream-pop" or "cottagecore" atmosphere.

Post Processing - A soft glow (bloom) and sun rays (god rays).

### UI and "Juice"

Kawaii/anime tropes like big star eyes. Extra animations that make an action feel rewarding.
